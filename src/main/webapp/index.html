Modeling the Knowledge Base

In the Brain, modeling is the graphical representation of the knowledge, their relationships with other knowledges ( parents and children ) and the topics in which the same is divided, all through a mental map. The modeling is part of the process of creating the knowledge base itself, and is synchronized with the robot's real base.

Model the knowledge base of a chatbot is not exactly a trivial task, since there is a set of variables that directly affect the effectiveness of a good human-robot interaction, such as the richness of the vocabulary of both, or the clarity and objectivity of messages of both, or the specialization or generalization expected by a user on a particular subject, etc. Also certain subjects may be too complex or too large to be handled by a robot.

Thinking about that, the Brain was designed to facilitate this task by adopting the strategy of "divide and conquer". By dividing, the knowledge can be modeled with very fine granularity, being reduced to several small knowledges ( which we call "atomic knowledge" ) where each is very specific, since focuses only on a particular aspect of a greater knowledge. Some of the advantages of this approach is to have a broader view on knowledge and be much easier to define all the topics covered by it. The result of this approach is a much more solid base and complete knowledge.

But the most important for the base to be well modeled is that the botmaster master each knowledge he wants to model. Must already have very clear in his mind what he wants to represent through the mental map of the Brain. Below is an outline of the modeling process:

  1. Set a target audience ;
  2. Set the topics of interest to this audience;
  3. Define the scope of topics ( knowledge to be modeled );
  4. For each item of scope ( all knowledge ):
    1. Understanding and knowledge that will be added;
    2. Define the relationships ( upward and downward );
    3. Set the topics.

Understanding the behavior of the knowledge base

The knowledge base has a very dynamic regarding their viewing behavior, but this dynamism is specifically designed to increase user productivity when modeling the base or creating lessons, and covers the following behaviors:

1. Selective Display

The knowledges are automatically displayed or hidden as relevant to clicked knowledge, so only the most relevant are displayed, so that the screen does not get tiring to display a lot of information and so the user can focus only on what really matters.

2. Visual Contextualization

When the user clicks a knowledge, the Brain automatically selects all its knowledge parents, leaving all the way from the root until it was clicked with a different color from the others. This is to facilitate the user's context in relation to knowledge that was selected.

3. Automatic Coloring

The color of knowledge automatically varies with the amount of internal knowledge that he has, starting light and going darker depending on the amount of internal knowledge increases.

The Knowledge

In the Brain, the knowledge is an object that encapsulates the descriptions about something . It can be part of a larger knowledge and can also be composed of other minor knowledges. Internally the knowledge is divided into topics.

To facilitate the processing of complex or comprehensive subjects , Brain uses the strategy of "Divide and Conquer". Thus how much fragmented and specific a knowledge is, ie, how much topics and knowledges children have that knowledge, the better is the domain of robot on it.

Adding a Knowledge

In the Brain, there are no isolated knowledge, all knowledge is necessarily connected to another, being the most basic root from which all derive.

  1. Right click on an existing knowledge;
  2. Click on "Add to Knowledge" option;
  3. Enter the name of new knowledge;
  4. Click "Save".

Renaming a Knowledge

  1. Right click on the Knowledge you want to update;
  2. Click on "Rename this Knowledge" option;
  3. Type the new name;
  4. Click "Save".

Deleting a Knowledge

Attention: When a knowledge is deleted, are also deleted in cascade all your children knowledge, its topics and its teachings.
  1. Right click on the knowledge that you wantto delete;
  2. Click the on "Delete this Knowledge" option;
  3. Click "Yes".

Topics

The Topic is a group of teachings, an internal division of a Knowledge, and is specific only to Knowledge which it belongs to. Thus, for creating, editing or deleting a Topic you must access this screen through the following steps:

  1. Right click on the Knowledge you want to add the Topic;
  2. Click on "Topics & Teachings" option;
  3. Wait loading the Topics panel.

Creating a Topic

  1. Click " Create";
  2. Type the name of the new Topic;
  3. Click " Save".
Renaming a Topic
  1. Click the Topic you want to update;
  2. Click " Update";
  3. Type the new name;
  4. Click " Save".

Deleting a Topic

Attention: When a Topic is deleted, are also deleted all its teachings.

  1. Click the Topic you want to delete;
  2. Click "Delete";
  3. Click "Yes".

At first, the robot should be seen as a child: initially has no knowledge, but starts to acquire it from the teachings that are passed to them.

The teaching is the smallest unit of knowledge passed to the robot, it is the statement itself of what he should do according to the text typed by the user. Are the teachings that actually prepare the robot to handle situations created by users.

The teaching is specific to just one topic, so it only makes sense for that topic to which it belongs to. Thus, to create, edit or delete a teaching, the user must access this screen through the following steps:

  1. Right click on the Knowledge you want to add the teachings;
  2. Click on " Topics & Teachings" option;
  3. Wait load the Topics panel;
  4. Click the Topic that will contains the teachings;
  5. Wait loading the Teachings panel.
